About

Reflecting my first semester as a UX graduate student at UMich.

Hello, I'm Aliyah

I'm currently a first–year UX graduate student at the University of Michigan, where I’ve spent my first semester exploring the foundations of design, research, and the creative process. Coming from an artistic background, I still think and work visually — sketching ideas, mapping concepts, and breaking big problems into simple shapes and lines.

This semester taught me how to connect that artistic instinct with structured UX methods. I learned how to plan research, analyze data, build flows, understand cognition, and design with intention. These projects represent the skills, mindsets, and lessons that shaped my first months as a UX designer in training.

Semester Highlights

  1. SI 500 — learned how to conduct interviews, analyze data, and translate findings into insights.
  2. SI 506 — Learned how to utilize Python.
  3. SI 539 — Learned how to build accessible websites using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  4. SI 588 — Learned about UX principals that build user experiences.
